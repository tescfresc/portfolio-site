MOLEZ
A MOLE-THEMED, WAVE-BASED, CO-OP SURVIVAL GAME
THE GAME
Set in a moles burrow, players must fight off waves of oncoming zombie badgers, completing mini-games to unlock doors and revive players. The badgers are hungry for moles, and they are relentless in their hunt for blood. MoleZ is a twin stick wave survival game inspired by games such as Call of Duty: Zombies and Minigore. The game uses the players smartphone as a controller, allowing for interesting mini-games and making co-op games with your friends even easier. The objective of the game is to survive to as high a wave as possible, unlocking rooms and even an upgrade shop as you make your way through the level.
MoleZ Gameplay

The main MoleZ game is developed to run on PC or Mac, and the controller app has been developed to run on any Android smartphone. This game was made as my final year project of a Ba(Hons) Game Design degree. The game and app were both built using Unity development software, as I have the most experience with using this software. The game uses Unityâ€™s (now deprecated) networking system, specifically the high-level API, to connect the mobile devices to the main game. The main game runs as a multiplayer server, and the smartphones connect directly as clients. Due to this, the controllers must be on the same network as the main game to allow a successful connection. A direct connection allows messages to be sent between the clients and server at high speed, with almost no latency on a high-quality connection.
USING A PHONE AS A CONTROLLER
The game is based around the idea of using a smartphone as a controller for a larger game, which has many benefits but also a few drawbacks. Taking these into full consideration, MoleZ manages to create an intuitive and responsive experience that allows the player to forget they're using a phone as a controller. A few of the benefits and drawbacks are outlined below.
    BENEFITS
  • Most people own a smartphone, so playing co-op games with your friends is even easier.
  • Content on the controller can be changed to reflect in game scenarios and each player can have their own unique content/control scheme.
  • Game can make use of the phones accelerometer to allow motion controls.
  • A touch screen allows for different types of input other than buttons, such as swipes or pinches.
    DRAWBACKS
  • No tactile feedback when pressing buttons, so can be hard to find specific buttons without looking at the screen.
  • Slight latency when inputting button presses.
  • Phone batteries deplete a lot quicker than a standard controller
  • Different phone screen sizes and operating systems make it hard to develop a single control scheme that works across all devices

PHONE MINIGAMES
One of the main ideas behind the concept of MoleZ was the ability to use the smartphone to have minigames that are ran on the smartphone controller that affect the main game in some way. I initially planned to have more varied minigames, but as a POC, the two I created make use of the possibilities of the phone in creative ways. The first minigame the players encounter is when they would like to clear the way to open up new areas of the map. This minigame involves looking at the screen and timing when to press the button to break all the rocks. Due to the way this is designed, it means that the player has to divert attention away from the main game and risk taking damage, to instead focus on the screen of their smartphone. This game scales in difficulty as more doorways are opened.
The door opening minigame
Solo reviving minigame.
The second minigame is used to revive players. If it is a multiplayer game, then the player who is downed and needs reviving has a prompt appear on their screen. Their phone vibrates, similar to a heartbeat, with a random interval. It is then up to them to communicate with the other player what the specific rhythm is, as the player who is reviving them has to move their phone in a CPR fashion in time with their heartbeat. This forces communcation between the players, and brings the game out of the screens and into the real world, taking advantage of the possibilities of the smartphone controller. For players who might have vibration disabled, a visual prompt is also displayed.
